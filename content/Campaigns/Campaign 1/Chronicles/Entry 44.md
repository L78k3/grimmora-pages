# Entry 44

## Day 32

The party rises early to prepare for their meeting with [[Elara Mythin|Elara]]. During breakfast, [[Mary-Ann]] receives a letter from her goddaughter, who expresses her joy at Mary-Ann's arrival and mentions a ball occurring tonight. The goddaughter currently lacks a chaperone, and while Mary-Ann is somewhat annoyed at the obligation to attend, [[Temerity]] is immediately interested in the event. Once everyone is ready, [[Lavender]] ensures the group is up and they head toward the [[Mages' Guild]] headquarters.

As they approach, [[Gage]] examines the converted temple with his scout’s eye, noting purposeful scratches and chips out of the stonework, indicating an attempt to remove original iconography. Lavender recognizes that the building was once a temple to a god now long forgotten, a history erased long before the mages took rule. [[Theren]] senses that the erasure was not merely physical; powerful magical wards have been placed to stop the influence of the temple's original owner. The phrase “As Above So Below” comes to his mind, and he senses his patron may know more about this, reflecting on how a god with fewer worshippers becomes less powerful.

They meet Elara outside, who is dressed in subdued finery. Upon entering the headquarters, they find a massive interior where purple, blue, and gold banners hang from the ceiling. Spells fly through the air, animals chirrup, and wards surround every surface. Student mages are everywhere, studying and discussing lore. At the reception desk, a tall high elf with a severe countenance frowns at her book when Elara announces an appointment under [[House Mythin|Mythin]], but eventually shakes her head and agrees to let them through. 

They follow Elara to floating platforms that transport them to different floors. When they reach the sixth floor, known as the [[Arch-mage's Sanctum|Sanctum]], where the most important research occurs, a set of defensive wards strikes. Lavender, [[Anaphel]], and Mary-Ann take psychic damage and are suddenly unable to move. Lavender manages to whisper the situation to Elara, who halts the platform and departs to find someone to adjust the wards. While Anaphel and Mary-Ann manage to free themselves from the ward's grip, Lavender remains rooted until Elara returns and places a hand on her shoulder, allowing her to move again with an apology for the guild's protections.

Entering the alchemists’ lab within the Sanctum, they encounter three distinct researchers. They see [[Miriam]], an older half-elf lady talking to a squirrel on her shoulder; a rake-thin human looking over a flame and adding liquids of different colors; and [[Thomas]], a heavily bearded dwarven man with rings in his beard and hair who is cracking open gems to peer inside. Elara admits she does not know which of the three is the specific "Alchemist" they seek. Gage observes the human with the colored flame - a gaunt man in his 50s with grey-streaked hair named [[Felix]]. He wears dark robes stained with ash that seems out of place, even for his work, and his movements are slow, precise, and measured.

Theren approaches Miriam. He notices her companion, a squirrel named [[Brian]], appears to be in pain. Miriam stirs and tastes a mixture before feeding it to the squirrel, which immediately appears brighter and healthier. Theren asks the healer about fey enchantments, explaining his own name curse. Miriam explains that such enchantments are incredibly tricky to undo and advises him to use a nickname for a while, suggesting the fey might eventually investigate why the curse is no longer active. Theren cringes at the thought of remaining "Swampy" for longer, realizing it has been a long time since he was called by his true name.

Lavender approaches Felix, and the [[Ring of Orris]] reacts by growing slightly colder. She informs Anaphel of the reaction, who in turn admits to feeling uneasy in the Sanctum. Nearby, Gage approaches Thomas. He notices that the gem the dwarf is slicing is filled with fractures and splinters under the blade. Thomas explains the roles of the lab members: he is a gemstone expert, Miriam is a healer, and Felix works with flame. Thomas is visibly suspicious of Gage and stops Mary-Ann when she tries to inspect his notes. When Gage asks about the "mountain’s breath," Thomas becomes even more evasive and suspicious.

Temerity speaks with Brian the squirrel, who explains he fell from a tree but Miriam made him better. Brian calls the bard "Timothy" and immediately begins forming megalomaniacal plans to take over the world under the name "Brimothy," much to Temerity’s delight. However, when Temerity asks about the building's history as a temple, his [[Temerity's Choker|choker]] reacts, injuring him badly. He manages to hide the damage from Miriam, who claims to know nothing of the building’s history.

Lavender approaches Felix again with Anaphel at her side. She asks about a lead from the [[Encoded Letters|letters]] and mentions she is a friend of [[Orris Locke|Orris]], pointedly asking where her brother is. Felix finds this alarming and replies that her brother is a charlatan. As Thomas and Miriam look on in shock, Felix suddenly attacks.

Temerity uses [Misty Step](https://2014.5e.tools/spells.html#misty%20step_phb) to teleport closer and tries an [Eldritch Blast](https://2014.5e.tools/spells.html#eldritch%20blast_phb) that misses, while Lavender slashes Felix with her greatsword. Thomas grabs gemstones and lobs them; one turns into a miniature flame elemental near Lavender, and another becomes an earth elemental near the door. Miriam assists Temerity by throwing healing tinctures. Felix mutters an incantation, creating a massive [Wall of Fire](https://2014.5e.tools/spells.html#wall%20of%20fire_phb) that bisects the room. Theren and [[Runcible]] rush through the flames to position themselves for an attack. Mary-Ann fires her crossbow, but the bolt pings off a column, and she retreats to cover. Gage hides behind a pillar, readying his bow.

Elara shouts that she is going for help and flees the room. Anaphel summons [Spirit Guardians](https://2014.5e.tools/spells.html#spirit%20guardians_phb). Temerity successfully dispels the wall of fire and inspires Lavender. Gage hits Felix with an arrow. Felix retaliates by throwing a flaming vial between Theren, Temerity, and Lavender, causing fire damage to all. Theren grants Runcible resistance to fire to ensure his familiar's survival. Felix attempts to use a caustic flask on Lavender, but Temerity intervenes with [Cutting Words](https://2014.5e.tools/classes.html#bard_phb,state:feature=s2-0~sub_lore_phb=b1). Thomas resurrects his fallen earth elemental using another gemstone. 

Miriam provides healing to Lavender and Runcible, while Felix takes damage from Anaphel’s guardians. Felix then releases a cloud of steam that injures Anaphel and throws an alchemical bomb. The explosion deals significant damage to Miriam and Thomas, knocks Theren unconscious, dispels Runcible, and destroys the earth elemental. Anaphel is severely hurt, and Lavender and Temerity take further damage. Mary-Ann’s next bolt is blocked by Felix’s [Shield](https://2014.5e.tools/spells.html#shield_phb) spell. Gage casts [Hunter's Mark](https://2014.5e.tools/spells.html#hunter's%20mark_phb), and his subsequent arrow blasts through the shield to hit the alchemist.

Felix drinks a healing potion while Anaphel readies their action. Temerity [Polymorphs](https://2014.5e.tools/spells.html#polymorph_phb) Anaphel into a [Giant Ape](https://2014.5e.tools/bestiary.html#giant%20ape_mm), causing the spirit guardians to dissipate. Inspired by Temerity, the giant ape smashes a fist into Felix. Lavender moves in to subdue the alchemist, and Felix passes out. Miriam rushes to Theren and uses [Cure Wounds](https://2014.5e.tools/spells.html#cure%20wounds_phb) to revive him.

Lavender instructs Miriam to wake Felix for interrogation. Temerity uses [Detect Thoughts](https://2014.5e.tools/spells.html#detect%20thoughts_phb) while Lavender questions him. Although Felix initially refuses to answer, Temerity detects that the alchemist is a servant of the [[Master of Ash]]. Felix admits he does not know Orris's current location, but reveals that Orris met with the [[Arch-mage]] in the Inner Sanctum three days ago. As he speaks, the Ring of Orris pulses, sending a debilitating cold through Lavender’s body. Feeling that something is fundamentally wrong, Lavender asks Felix if his master can hear them. Felix claims the Master sees everything, to which Lavender replies that the Master will now know she is coming for him.
