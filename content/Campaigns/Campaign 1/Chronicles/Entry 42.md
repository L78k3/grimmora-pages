# **Day 30 (continued)**

[[Port Adal]] serves as the primary connection port for the capital city, with [[Adalens]] situated only a couple of hours away. [[Lavender]] knows that [[Adalens]] is a guild-run city governed by mages. The heart of the city is a massive, old temple that has been repurposed as the [[Mages' Guild|Mages' Guild]] arch-mage’s headquarters. The arch-mage is an elected figure, overseeing several powerful guilds: Mages, Merchants, Artisans, Scholars, Navigators, and Hospitallers. While there is no central religion and freedom of worship exists, religious practice is often viewed as frivolous by the citizenry. The cityscape is dominated by a colossal floating island, [[Adalens Ul-Maht]], featuring a towering spire that penetrates the clouds. It remains unknown what transpires upon that floating rock. [[Adalens]] is a true metropolis - a constructed, non-organic city where wealth disparity is less obvious than in other regions, though unscrupulous areas still exist alongside legitimate ones.

Deciding to move immediately to [[Adalens]] rather than lingering in [[Port Adal]], the party is accosted by a large half-orc and a slender half-elf in blue city watch uniforms as they leave the docks. They are invited to register at the harbour master's office, joining a long queue. [[Lavender]] whispers a warning to the group to use false names. While they wait, they recall [[Captain Voss|Voss]]'s advice: [[Port Adal]] is run by the Guild, and they should seek information at the Saltwater Exchange or goods at [[The Bazaar]]. [[Captain Voss|Voss]] also warned that the five ruling families have eyes everywhere: [[House Nightbrook]] (information), [[House Silverwind]] (artisans), [[House Crestwood]] (merchants), [[House Mythin]] (scholars), and [[House Kinver]] (military), the latter being an offshoot of [[House Locke]].

When they reach the clerk, [[Lavender]] registers the group as [[Mary-Ann]]’s security detail using aliases: [[Gage]] is "Littlerock Sprockett," [[Lavender]] is "Eloise Rosewood," [[Anaphel]] is "Lyra Frostwinter," and [[Temerity]] is "Diffidence." A panicked [[Theren]] introduces himself as "Swampy." The clerk is suspicious of [[Theren]], but [[Mary-Ann]] clarifies they are travelling companions as well as security. [[Temerity's Choker|Temerity's choker]] tightens painfully as he lies, though he hides the discomfort. Waved through into the port proper, [[Lavender]] pays for a carriage to the capital.

Upon arrival, the 30-foot white walls of [[Adalens]] rise before them. The main gate is wrought metal, radiating a sense of magic, and is manned by guards in blue and gold city watch attire. A guard named [[Gregor Withwhile]] stops them, demanding a one-gold entry tax per person. [[Mary-Ann]], unimpressed, demands a receipt and Gregor's name for later verification. [[Theren]] intuits the guard is being truthful. Inside, the bustling city proves overwhelming for [[Theren]] and [[Aurius]], who cowers behind [[Temerity]], though [[Mary-Ann]], [[Lavender]], and [[Gage]] feel comfortable in the urban environment.

[[Lavender]] is eager to use the [[Sending Stone|sending stone]] in an alley, but [[Mary-Ann]] advises heading to a tavern first. They choose the [[Guild’s Rest]], a three-storey wooden building in the Guild quarter. Inside, the atmosphere is warm and welcoming, lit by multiple hearths. A weather-worn dwarven woman with facial scars manages the bar. [[Mary-Ann]] haggles for rooms on the third floor. [[Gage]] intuits the establishment's respectability and decides to keep his links to the [[Adalens Thieves' Guild|Thieves’ Guild]] a secret. [[Mary-Ann]] and [[Gage]] head to the bathhouse, which is calm and relaxed. [[Mary-Ann]] uses the jacuzzi while [[Gage]] enters the ice pool, where he encounters a hairy man named [[Cornelius]] who breaks rocks.

Back in their quarters, the party notices [[Anaphel]] has been uncharacteristically silent, staring at the wall. [[Lavender]] senses something is wrong and confronts them, admitting she was ready to sacrifice herself during the sea battle. [[Anaphel]], angry and fearful, argues that they would never have allowed the loss of both [[Auriel]] and [[Lavender]]. After [[Lavender]] apologises, [[Anaphel]] confesses their deep feelings for her. Later, the group gathers to use the [[Sending Stone]]. [[Mary-Ann]] declares she is "fully in" for the coming danger. They coordinate a rendezvous with [[Orris Locke|Orris]], choosing a location outside the [[Blooming Lovely]] florist at noon on the 31st. They sign the message "LX3" and receive a brief reply: "Understood."

# **Day 31**

[[Lavender]] sends [[Gage]] to scout the temple district and purchase supplies. He finds shrines ranging from small flower-enclosed altars to large buildings, including a prominent temple to [[Pellor]]. During his search, he finds an unremarkable closed door with ice crystals forming at the base, but decides not to investigate further. He is unable to find an advertised shrine to [[Sargonnas|Sargonis]]. At [[The Bazaar]], he visits an ancient apothecary and purchases two minor [[health potions|healing potions]] and snacks from the oldest man he has ever seen.

Meanwhile, [[Lavender]] visits [[Theren]] in his room to discuss the "thread of fate." She asks if he is a half-moon elf; [[Theren]] nods uncomfortably, confessing he knows little of his mother or elven heritage. [[Lavender]] suggests their meeting was no coincidence given [[Auriel]]'s connection to the moon, leaving [[Theren]] to consider if he was born for a purpose.

The party heads to [[Blooming Lovely]]. [[Temerity]] notices six different people attempting, and failing, to look inconspicuous nearby. [[Orris Locke|Orris]] is absent, but a halfling in a cloak delivers a message to [[Mary-Ann]] instructing them to meet at the [[House Mythin]] headquarters. [[Mary-Ann]] recalls that one of the [[Encoded Letters]] regarding "The Alchemist" was from [[Orris Locke|Orris]] to [[Elara Mythin]]. They make their way to the affluent estates of the ruling families.

At the massive [[House Mythin]] estate, a goblin butler named [[Grawlock]] answers the door. He informs them that Master Locke has not been seen for days but eventually invites them in, as the mistress is expecting them. A small bugbear guides them through corridors shimmering with magic. While [[Gage]] stakes out the building from a coffee shop and [[Theren]] and [[Temerity]] keep watch, [[Lavender]], [[Mary-Ann]], and [[Anaphel]] meet [[Elara Mythin]] in a beautiful sitting room.

Lady [[Elara Mythin]] reveals that [[Orris Locke|Orris]] had to flee and lay low because someone is onto him. It was she who had used the [[Sending Stone]] to reply. [[Orris Locke|Orris]] left a message to seek "The Alchemist" within the [[Mages' Guild]], an identity Elara has narrowed down to three possibilities likely working in the [[Arch-mage's Sanctum]]. Elara pledges her allegiance to the side of knowledge and agrees to meet the party at the sanctum tomorrow morning at 8:00 AM to find the Alchemist together. [[Lavender]] accedes, now certain that [[Orris Locke|Orris]] is a member of the [[Mages' Guild]].
