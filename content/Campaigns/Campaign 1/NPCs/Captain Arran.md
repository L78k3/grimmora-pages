**Captain Arran** is a high-ranking military officer within the [[Durvish City]] Guard who was instrumental in initiating the investigation into [[Lady Bromelia]]'s kidnapping.

## Biography & Personality
Captain Arran is a figure of significant authority and reputation within the [[Avalonean Empire]]. As a military leader in Durvish City, he oversees local security and investigations into high-profile crimes, such as the abduction of nobility. He is recognized by other regional guards, suggesting his name carries weight beyond the walls of his own city.

Though his direct interactions with the party have been limited, he is the primary authority who sanctioned their pursuit of the "Man in Black." He is practical and mission-oriented, willing to employ capable adventurers to handle threats that standard guard patrols might miss.

## Campaign History
* **[[Entry 01]] (The Wedding Investigation):** Following the abduction of [[Lady Bromelia]], Captain Arran was involved in the immediate response and investigation within [[Durvish City]].
* **[[Entry 02]] (Credentials in Avalon):** Upon arriving at [[Avalon]], the party finds their way blocked by guards at the south-west gate. [[Lavender]] successfully gains entry by claiming they were sent by Captain Arran of Durvish City to pursue the culprits who kidnapped the bride, demonstrating the reach of his authority.
* **[[Entry 13]] (The Quest Source):** During a discussion with [[Lady Bromelia]], the party reaffirms that it was Captain Arran who originally sent them to investigate the necromantic goings-on related to her disappearance.

## Key Relationships
* **[[Lavender]]:** Though they serve different cities, Lavender is aware of his reputation and utilizes his name as a powerful diplomatic tool to bypass bureaucratic obstacles.
* **[[Lady Bromelia]]:** As a military officer of her home city, he was the primary investigator responsible for her "rescue" before the true nature of her disappearance was revealed.

## Notable Equipment
* **Officer's Seal:** While not directly seen, his authority is often represented by official credentials or the recognition of his rank by imperial soldiers.
