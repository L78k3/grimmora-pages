**Thomas** is a heavily bearded dwarven alchemist and gemstone expert working within the [[Arch-mage's Sanctum]] of the [[Mages' Guild]] in [[Adalens]].

## Biography & Personality
Thomas is a stout dwarf characterized by a heavy beard and hair adorned with rings. As a resident researcher in the [[Arch-mage's Sanctum]], he operates a specialized workstation equipped with a pestle and mortar for crushing materials and uses a stool to reach his equipment. He is an expert in the study and faceting of gemstones, though he has noted that some recent specimens contain unusual fractures.

Personality-wise, Thomas is highly suspicious and guarded around strangers. He is protective of his research, once physically stopping [[Mary-Ann]] from inspecting his notes. He is also notably evasive; when questioned by [[Gage]] regarding the "mountain’s breath," his suspicion and defensive behavior increased significantly.

## Campaign History
* **[[Entry 44]] (The Sanctum Skirmish):** First encountered by the party in the alchemists' lab. When a conflict erupted following [[Felix]]’s sudden aggression, Thomas initially supported his colleague by lobbing alchemical gemstones that transformed into miniature flame and earth elementals. During the height of the battle, he was caught in the blast of Felix's alchemical bomb, suffering significant fire damage. After Felix was subdued, Thomas remained in the lab and was present for the subsequent interrogation.

## Key Relationships
* **[[Miriam]]:** A colleague in the lab; Thomas identified her as the team's healer.
* **[[Felix]]:** A lab colleague; Thomas initially fought on his side during the Sanctum skirmish before Felix's true allegiance to the [[Master of Ash]] was revealed.
* **[[Gage]]:** The subject of Thomas’s intense suspicion during the party's initial investigation of the lab.

## Notable Equipment
* **Alchemical Gemstones:** Specialized gems that Thomas can lob to summon elemental constructs, such as miniature flame or earth elementals.
* **Pestle and Mortar:** Standard tools of his trade used for crushing alchemical components.
