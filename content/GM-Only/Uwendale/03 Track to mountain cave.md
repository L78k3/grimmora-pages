## Scene Overview

The party sets out from the farm or borderlands, following the trail of kidnappers into the rugged Stonespine Peaks. The journey is urgent and perilous: the terrain is treacherous, time is short, and the threat of enemy scouts or wild beasts looms. The goal is to track the abductors to their mountain cave lair before harm befalls the captives.

## Objectives

- Primary: Track the kidnappers’ trail through the mountains to the cave before they can relocate or harm the prisoners.
- Secondary: Avoid natural hazards, enemy scouts, and possible wyvern activity; gather information about the cave’s defences or layout.

## Key NPCs & Creatures

- Kidnappers: Noxian or Freljordian agents (or their local allies), moving quickly with their captives.
- Possible wild threats: Mountain predators or a wyvern on the wing.
- Captives: Villagers or fellow soldiers, potentially leaving clues or attempting to slow their captors.

## Setting & Atmosphere

- The Stonespine Peaks: steep, rocky, and cold, with narrow trails, sudden drop-offs, and the ever-present risk of rockslides.
- Sparse vegetation, howling winds, and distant animal cries create a sense of isolation and urgency.
- Occasional signs of passage - broken branches, discarded items, or hurried footprints in the mud or snow.

## Challenges & Obstacles

- Survival/Tracking Challenge: PCs must use skills like Survival, Perception, and Stealth to follow the trail, avoid hazards, and remain undetected.
- Environmental Hazards: Loose scree, sudden storms, or precarious ledges test the party’s teamwork and resourcefulness.
- Optional Combat/Encounter: Ambush by enemy scouts, or a territorial beast drawn by the scent of blood or noise.

## Clues & Rewards

- Clues: Dropped possessions, coded markers, or signs of struggle that confirm the party is on the right path and hint at the cave’s defences.
- Rewards: Opportunity to catch the kidnappers off guard, gain advantage on the upcoming cave encounter, or rescue a straggling captive.

## Complications & Twists

- The trail splits or is deliberately obscured, forcing the party to make a risky decision or split up.
- Signs that the cave is not only a hideout, but also houses something more dangerous (e.g., a wyvern nest or enemy ritual site).
- Weather worsens, adding urgency and risk to the journey.

## Transition

The party arrives at the mouth of the mountain cave, battered but determined. They have a moment to plan their approach before entering, setting the stage for a tense rescue operation and confrontation within the kidnappers’ lair.
