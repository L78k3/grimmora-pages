## Scene Overview

The recruits begin their day at the Uwendale military outpost, a remote Demacian settlement nestled in the Stonespine Peaks. The morning routine is interrupted by urgent news - either a nearby farm is under attack, or several villagers have gone missing. Lieutenant Magnus Crownguard gathers the squad for immediate action, setting the tone for a day of high alert and frontier justice.

## Objectives

- Primary: Muster, receive orders, and respond to the immediate threat (attack or abduction).
- Secondary: Establish relationships with fellow recruits and Lieutenant Crownguard; demonstrate readiness and teamwork; gather rumours or information from villagers.

## Key NPCs & Creatures

- Lieutenant Magnus Crownguard: Commanding officer, stern but pragmatic.
- Fellow recruits (other PCs): Recently completed basic training, bonded through shared hardship.
- Villagers: Suspicious of magic, reliant on the military for protection.
- Possible early threat: Messenger or panicked survivor (not a direct combatant yet).

## Setting & Atmosphere

- The outpost is built of stone and timber, with petricite in its construction to ward against magic.
- Morning mist hangs over the peaks, livestock bleat in distant pens, and the clang of the smithy echoes.
- The air is tense; rumours of wyvern sightings and border skirmishes circulate among the troops.

## Challenges & Obstacles

- Skill challenge: Rapidly donning gear, organizing squads, and making sense of conflicting reports.
- Social challenge: Navigating suspicion or rivalry among recruits; calming frightened villagers.
- Decision point: Which threat to address first - attack at the farm or missing villagers?

## Clues & Rewards

- Clues: Signs of a struggle, tracks leading into the mountains, or a cryptic warning from a survivor.
- Rewards: Early chance to earn respect from the lieutenant and villagers; possible minor equipment upgrades (extra rations, a petricite token).

## Complications & Twists

- The threat is more complex than it appears: the attack may be a diversion for a kidnapping, or vice versa.
    
- Hints of Noxian or Freljordian involvement, or evidence that magic may be at play despite local suspicion.
    

## Transition

The party is deployed - either to defend the farm, pursue kidnappers into the mountains, or split up. Their choices here set the stage for the branching middle of the adventure, leading to the next encounter in the diamond structure.
