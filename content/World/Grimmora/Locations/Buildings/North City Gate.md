# North City Gate

> [!info] Visited
> **Location:** [[Avalon]], northern perimeter
> **Region:** [[Avalonean Empire]]

> [!warning] Affected by Curse
> Guards here experienced memory suppression similar to [[Gorg]]'s condition.

## Description

The North City Gate serves as one of the main entry points to [[Avalon]], controlling access from the northern territories. It's a fortified position with guard barracks nearby and serves as a checkpoint for trade caravans and travelers.

## Location

Located at [[Avalon]]'s northern perimeter, the gate marks the transition between the outer roads and the city's inner defensive wall. It's the primary access point for those traveling from [[The Frost]] and other northern regions.

## Purpose

- Primary gate for trade caravans entering from northern routes
- Security checkpoint for travelers
- Military defensive position
- Guard station and barracks location

## The Memory Suppression Incident

Guards stationed at the North City Gate experienced episodes of **memory suppression** similar to what affected [[Gorg]] at [[The Tavern]] in [[Mightrest]].

### Pattern Recognition
This incident was significant because it revealed:
- The memory manipulation wasn't limited to a single individual
- Multiple locations were affected by similar phenomena
- A broader magical curse or anomaly might be at work across Grimmora
- The pattern extended beyond [[The Frost]] into the [[Avalonean Empire]]

### Nature of the Effect
Like [[Gorg]]'s inability to process the concept of churches, the guards here experienced similar cognitive blocks or memory gaps. The exact nature and trigger of this suppression remains unclear.

## Strategic Importance

As a major entry point to the capital:
- Heavy traffic from northern trade routes
- First line of defense against northern threats
- Information hub for news from [[The Frost]]
- Critical for city security

## Our Experience

We encountered the first reports of memory suppression here, providing our earliest evidence that the phenomenon affecting [[Gorg]] extended beyond a single individual. This was a crucial piece of evidence in understanding the scope of the magical influence or curse affecting the region.

The guards' condition paralleled what we'd seen before, suggesting a systematic pattern rather than isolated incidents.

## Mysteries

- What triggered the memory suppression in the guards?
- Is the curse localized to specific locations or more widespread?
- Who or what is responsible for this magical effect?
- Are there other affected sites we haven't discovered?
